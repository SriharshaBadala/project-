Traditional festival food of Assam

Bihu

Bihu is one of Assam's most prominent cultural events, celebrating the changing seasons. Learn about the celebration of Bihu and its types. During harvest season, Assam celebrates its national festival, "Bihu". The Sanskrit word Bihu signifies "to pray for blessings and prosperity." So, on the festival day, Farmers and local people pray to God for a successful harvest on Bihu. This marks the arrival of spring and the onset of the harvesting season. The festival is a way of thanking the gods for a bountiful harvest and seeking their blessings for the upcoming year. Secondly, Rongali Bihu is a celebration of the rich cultural heritage of Assam.

Significance of Bihu Festival

Bihu is celebrated to mark the Assamese New Year and the harvest season. It expresses gratitude to nature, livestock, and deities for prosperity and abundance. The traditional food prepared during Bihu is:

Bora Saulor Pitha

A famous sweet dish prepared during Bihu, Bora Saulor Pitha is made with rice and jaggery. The rice batter is spread in a circular manner on a tawa, post which a coconut and sugar mixture is added to it. The rice crepe is then folded on to the mixture, thus creating a roll. It is made out of rice and black sesame. Bora saul is used in various other Assamese dishes, such as jolpan (snacks) and Pitha (rice cake or pancake). Boiled bora saul is served as jolpan with curd or milk, jaggery or sugar. Soaked and ground bora saul is used in preparing pitha.
