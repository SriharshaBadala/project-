Traditional festival food of Jharkhand

Karam Puja

Karam (colloquially Karma) is a harvest festival celebrated in Jharkhand. It is dedicated to the worship of Karam-Devta (Karam-Lord/God), the god of power, youth and youthfulness. It celebrated for good harvest and health. Unmarried girls fast and grow seedlings for 7-9 days. Then next day, groups of young villagers go to the jungle and collect wood, fruits, and flowers. These are required during the puja (worship) of the Karam God.

Significance of Karam Puja

Karam Puja is one of the most important tribal festivals of Jharkhand and eastern India. It is celebrated to worship Karam Devta, the god of youth and strength. The puja is symbolic of prosperity, community bonding, fertility, and environmental reverence.

The Karam tree branch is brought from the forest and worshipped with traditional songs and dances. Fasting unmarried girls and community participation make the festival an embodiment of social harmony and cultural pride.

The Traditional festival food of Karam Puja is:

Handia

A delightful beverage from the area, handia requires nearly a week to create. In actuality, the handia is a fermented rice beer, made from rice that has been cooked with over twenty herbs. It is produced particularly for special events, like the paddy harvesting celebration Karma Puja. The Munda and Santhal tribes regard it as a holy beverage as well.
