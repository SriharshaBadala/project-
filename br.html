Traditional festival food of Bihar

Chhath puja

Bihar's most famous and original festival is Chhath Puja, celebrated six days after Diwali. The festival is marked by devotion, fasting, and the distribution of sweets and fruits. The Puja is concluded with a holy dip in the Ganges river on the final day.

×

Significance of Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is a Vedic Hindu festival dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) and his wife Usha. Devotees offer prayers to thank Surya for sustaining life and to seek his blessings. It symbolizes purity, devotion, and gratitude. It also emphasizes ecological awareness and is celebrated with strict rituals including fasting and bathing in holy rivers.

Traditional festival food of Chhath Puja:

Thekua

During Chhath Puja, foods include Thekua (sweet cookies made with wheat flour, jaggery, and ghee), fruits, kheer (rice pudding), puris (deep-fried bread), and chana dal (Bengal gram). Thekua is the most popular prasad during Chhath Puja. It is made from ghee, wheat flour, dry fruits, and sugar. The ingredients are made into a dough, which is later shaped into small tikkis and deep-fried. It is offered to Lord Sun during the puja.

Significance

Since cleanliness is a major aspect of this celebration, devotees clean the wheat grains at home and get the flour specifically for the bhog meal. Mango wood is used to create a flame, and an earthen burner is used to make this prasad, which is seen as a vital part of the celebration.
