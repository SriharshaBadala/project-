Why is Sankranthi Celebrated in January?

Sankranthi marks the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Makara (Capricorn), called Makara Sankranti. This astronomical event signifies the end of winter solstice and the beginning of longer days. The festival is celebrated to honor the Sun God (Surya) and to express gratitude for a bountiful harvest.

Bhogi

During Bhogi, dishes like Bobbatlu and Pulihora are prepared. Tharpanam is offered to ancestors which includes vada with sesame chutney, moong dal, amla chutney, and payasam.
