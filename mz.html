Traditional festival food of Mizoram

Chapchur Kut

The Chapchar Kut is a festival of Mizoram, India. It is celebrated during March after completion of their most arduous task of jhum operation i.e., jungle-clearing (clearing of the remnants of burning). It is a spring festival celebrated with great favour and gaiety.

Significance of Chapchar Kut

Chapchar Kut marks the end of arduous jungle-clearing for jhum cultivation and expresses gratitude to nature. It is a time of rest, music, dance, and communal joy. It reflects Mizoram‚Äôs deep connection to agriculture, seasonal cycles, and community solidarity.

Traditional festival food of Chapchur Kut:

Smoked pork

The dish cooked for the occasion is smoked pork. In the dish vawksa rep chhum, the pork is typically boiled with green leafy vegetables such as mustard leaves or cabbage. A special dish called Sawhchiar, which is prepared with rice and meat is cooked, and is like a porridge that resembles risotto.
