Traditional festival food of Jammu and Kashmir

Hemis

Hemis Festival in Kashmir is one of the mysterious celebrations. The festival honors Lord Padmasambhava, also known as Guru Rinpoche in the area, and is held every 12 years at the Hemis Monastery in Hemis. A Kashmir festival that is very popular in Ladakh is the Hemis festival which is celebrated as the triumph of good over evil.

Significance of Hemis Festival

The Hemis Festival is a vibrant cultural and spiritual celebration in Ladakh that honors the birth of Guru Padmasambhava, the founder of Tibetan Buddhism. It symbolizes the triumph of good over evil. Monks perform sacred mask dances (Cham), and the Hemis Monastery comes alive with chants, rituals, and offerings. The festival serves to preserve ancient traditions and attract global visitors to Ladakhi heritage.

Thukpa

It is a combination of Tibetan-style noodles with vegetables or chicken stew in clear soup. There are several varieties of thukpa, the most popular being gyathuk, pathug, drethug, bakhthu, which use cowrie (shaped like macaroni but made from wheat flour), and thenthuk, which is made of flat noodles.
