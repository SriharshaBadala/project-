Significance of Pongal Festival

Pongal is celebrated in Tamil Nadu to mark the beginning of the harvest season. It is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya), thanking him for the bountiful harvest. The festival symbolizes prosperity, gratitude, and the harmonious bond between humans and nature. The overflowing of milk in the Pongal pot signifies abundance and joy.

Pongal

Pongal is a South Indian dish of rice cooked in boiling milk. Its preparation is the main custom associated with Pongal festival. As part of Tamil cuisine, varieties include venn (hot) pongal, sakkarai (sweet) pongal, kozhi (chicken) pongal, and sanyasi pongal.

Significance:

Pongal is associated with the Pongal festival whose name means "to boil over". The festival thanks the Sun deity for the sunlight that makes the rice harvest possible. Therefore, the tradition calls for offering the fresh harvest of rice cooked in boiling milk to the deity.
