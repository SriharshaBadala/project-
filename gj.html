Traditional festival foods of Gujarat

Navratri

The first thing that pops up in one's mind when they talk about festivals of Gujarat is Navratri. The most celebrated festival in Gujarat is commemorated gleefully in other parts of India as well. Three things sum up Navratri; Dandiya, Garba and lots of fun. It is one of the most celebrated festivals that is spread across 9 days worshipping Goddess Durga and immersing her idol in the holy water on 10th day.

The traditional foods prepared during Navratri are:

  • Day 1 – Sev Khamani: Made with chana dal and some spices and garnished with sev and pomegranate.
    • Sev Khamani (~150g)
    • 280 kcal
    • Carbs: 30g
    • Protein: 10g
    • Fat: 12g
  • Day 2 – Khandvi: A smooth and melting dish made with curd and gram flour, shaped into rolls.
    • Khandvi (~150g)
    • 200 kcal
    • Carbs: 15g
    • Protein: 6g
    • Fat: 10g
  • Day 3 – Sabudana Khichdi: Made with sabudana, peanuts, potatoes and spices, garnished with coriander.
    • Sabudana Khichdi (~150g)
    • 350 kcal
    • Carbs: 45g
    • Protein: 3g
    • Fat: 15g
  • Day 4 – Gol Papdi: A jaggery and flour-based sweet, often made during Navratri for its energizing value.
    • Gol Papdi (~150g)
    • 420 kcal
    • Carbs: 40g
    • Protein: 5g
    • Fat: 20g
  • Day 5 – Fafda: A crispy fried snack made with gram flour and carom seeds. Enjoyed with chutney.
    • Fafda (~150g)
    • 310 kcal
    • Carbs: 35g
    • Protein: 6g
    • Fat: 12g
  • Day 6 – Dhokla: A steamed savory cake made with fermented gram flour and tempered with mustard seeds.
    • Dhokla (~150g)
    • 240 kcal
    • Carbs: 30g
    • Protein: 8g
    • Fat: 8g
  • Day 7 – Biranj: A sweet ghee rice flavored with cardamom and dry fruits.
    • Biranj (~150g)
    • 320 kcal
    • Carbs: 40g
    • Protein: 4g
    • Fat: 12g
  • Day 8 – Batata nu Shaak: A spiced potato curry made with sesame, mustard seeds, and curry leaves.
    • Batata nu Shaak (~150g)
    • 270 kcal
    • Carbs: 28g
    • Protein: 3g
    • Fat: 14g
  • Day 9 – Sama Dry Fruits Pulao: A fragrant rice dish made from sama rice and mixed nuts.
    • Sama Pulao (~150g)
    • 390 kcal
    • Carbs: 50g
    • Protein: 7g
    • Fat: 16g

Significance

The significance of Navratri is offering devotion to Goddess Amba (Durga), who is believed to exist in many forms. Navratri’s fasting transcends mere dietary restrictions; it embodies a deep sense of devotion and discipline. Devotees meticulously adhere to a range of food limitations, abstaining from grains, specific vegetables, table salt, and certain spices.
