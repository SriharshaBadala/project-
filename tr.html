Traditional festival food of Tripura

Kharachi Puja

Kharachi Puja is one of the most popular festivals in Tripura. It is a week-long royal puja which falls in the month of July on the eighth day of the new moon and attracts thousands of people. This festival is celebrated at Agartala in the temple premises of Fourteen Gods. Kharachi puja is basically done to worship the earth. The puja is performed to wash out the sins and to clean the post-menstrual phase of Mother Earth.

Significance of Kharachi Puja

Kharachi Puja is a highly revered royal ritual observed in Tripura, primarily to worship the Mother Earth. It is believed to cleanse the sins and impurities of the earth, especially symbolizing purification after her menstrual cycle. The puja signifies reverence to natural forces and fertility. During this sacred celebration, various traditional foods like Muya Awandru are prepared using local and culturally important ingredients like bamboo shoots, fermented fish, and rice flour. This not only connects the community to their roots but also celebrates Tripuri culinary heritage.

The food offered during Kharachi puja:

Muya Awandru

People offer wide variety of sweets and the sacrificial meat to god. During Kharachi puja in Tripura, a special dish called "Muya Awandru" is prepared. It's a traditional Tripuri dish made with pork, rice, and bamboo shoots. One popular dish that relies heavily on the use of bamboo is Muya Awundru which is made with a rice flour based gravy cooked with fermented fish and varieties of local indigenous vegetables, garnished with haori basil leaves.
