Traditional festival food of Karnataka

Ugadi

Ugadi is new year's day according to Hindu calendar. The first day of each year is called 'Ugadi'. The word Ugadi can be split into two ‚Äî Uga means Course of stars and Adi means Starting. The day is observed by drawing colourful patterns on the floor called Muggulu, mango leaf decorations on doors called torana, buying and giving gifts such as new clothes, giving charity to the poor, oil massages followed by special baths, preparing and sharing a special food called pachadi.

Significance of Ugadi Festival

Ugadi marks the beginning of a new year in Karnataka and other southern states. It symbolizes the cycle of life, filled with emotions of joy, sorrow, anger, fear, disgust, and surprise‚Äîrepresented in the six tastes of the Ugadi Pachadi. It reminds people to embrace all aspects of life with balance and gratitude. The festival is associated with renewal, cleanliness, charity, and hope for a prosperous year ahead.

Ugadi pachadi is a special food prepared during Ugadi. The pachadi is a notable festive food that combines all flavors ‚Äì sweet, sour, salty, bitter, astringent and piquant. In Telugu and Kannada Hindu traditions, it is a symbolic reminder that one must expect all flavors of experiences in the coming new year and make the most of them.
